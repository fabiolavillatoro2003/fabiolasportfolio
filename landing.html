About me.

You can call me Fabi.

"What is?"

I spent my freshman year of college at Binghamton University as a Philosophy, Politics, and Law Major, where I immersed myself in a heavy philosophy-based curriculum. Since transferring to Columbia, my interest in philosophy-adjacent subjects has only been magnified, owing to the widely technical courseload I have taken on.

A widely known debate that in which I am particularly interested in is abortion. Diving into the argumentative meat and bones is fascinating. My favorite arguments on this issue include Judith Thomson's Defense of Abortion (famously), and Donald Marquis Future Like Ours counterargument. I have formed my own contentions and modifications of Thomson's violinist argument, and equally, I have built a counterargument towards Marquis' antiabortionist argument. Let me know if you are interested in discussing either! Philosophy is one of the most invigorating topics of conversation!

Am

Federer over Nadal!

"Though we may be learned by another's knowledge, we can never be wise but by our own experience." - Michel De Montaigne

Since finishing the 2022-2023 academic year, I have become a regular at my local tennis courts, and an attentive watcher of current and past tournaments. The motivation? None. What I like about having taking on this hobby is the lack of plan I had to do so. Thus, I express my spontaneity through Montaigne, as what makes this new hobby so fulfilling is that I am only experiencing it as deep as I know how. Just as I do not seek to be a pro, I do not plan on dropping my racket after a couple bad serves. That being said... 'M' or 'W'? Hill or valley?
